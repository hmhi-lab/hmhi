About HMHI

HMHI stands for Human-Machine Harmony for Infrastructure. We explore how people and machines can work together to make infrastructure smarter, safer, and more resilient.

The name "Human-Machine Harmony" reflects our core belief: that future infrastructure systems must be designed not just for automation, but for seamless collaboration between humans and intelligent machines.

Our lab focuses on the intersection of human cognition, AI, and infrastructure operations. We design systems that support decision-making and improve coordination in high-stakes, complex environments.

Our work spans:

  • Human-centered design and human factors engineering
  • Artificial intelligence and machine learning
  • Cognitive modeling and explainable systems
  • Immersive training and simulation environments

From nuclear power to transportation, and from urban centers to remote communities, we aim to create infrastructure solutions that are adaptive, transparent, and deeply human-aware.
