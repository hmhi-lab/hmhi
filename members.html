HMHI Human-Machine Harmony for Infrastructure

Lab Members

Meet the people behind HMHI — students, postdocs, and collaborators working together to shape the future of human-machine collaboration in infrastructure systems.

Postdoctoral Researchers

Pengkun Liu

pengkunl@andrew.cmu.edu

Pengkun is a Postdoctoral Researcher at Carnegie Mellon University, researching human-AI collaboration of inspection for infrastructure. He received his Ph.D. from Carnegie Mellon University. His innovative approach involves combining human and artificial intelligence to create cognitive assistants capable of transferring the valuable knowledge of senior workers to new generations, ultimately improving efficiency, safety, and sustainability in infrastructure management.

Jinfeng Lou

jinfengl@andrew.cmu.edu

Jinfeng is a Carnegie Bosch Fellow at Carnegie Mellon University, researching human-AI collaboration for infrastructure resilience and emergency response. He received his Ph.D. from the University of Hong Kong and has led and participated in multiple international research projects on smart construction, modular buildings, and digital infrastructure systems.

PhD Students

Miaosi Dong

miaosid@andrew.cmu.edu

Miaosi Dong is a Ph.D. candidate researching building production and cyber-physical systems. She develops flexible manufacturing processes with production digital twins, applies AI/ML to optimize advanced working packages, and enhances human-AI collaboration. Her interdisciplinary work integrates digital twins and automated learning to boost construction efficiency and decision-making.

Ava Jahan Biglari

Avaj@andrew.cmu.edu

Ava is a Ph.D. candidate at Carnegie Mellon University, where she also earned her M.Sc. in Civil & Environmental Engineering. She holds a B.Sc. in Civil Engineering from the University of Tehran. Her research focuses on enhancing the safety and efficiency of heavy-duty vehicle fleets through digital twins, physics-informed machine learning and human-AI collaboration.

Seongeun Park

seongeup@andrew.cmu.edu

Seongeun's research focuses on human-AI collaboration for decision-making in high-risk infrastructure systems such as nuclear power and remote civil operations. She develop knowledge-augmented AI systems, using Graph Retrieval-Augmented Generation (GraphRAG) to enhance anomaly recognition, fault diagnosis, and procedural understanding.

Joonsun Hwang

joonsunh@anderew.cmu.edu

Joonsun's research focuses on enhancing human-AI collaborative decision-making and improving workplace in various civil engineering scenarios. He received his Bachelor of Architecture from Seoul National University.
